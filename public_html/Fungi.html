Introduction To Fungi

Structure And Function Of Fungi

Fungi are eukaryotes and may have one cell nucleus or multiple cell nuclei. When it comes to fungi, we probably think of delicious mushrooms on the market or mold on bread. These elemental cycles of fungi and the biological world also play an important role, especially the decomposition of cellulose or lignin.

In medicine

  1. The first antibiotic-penicillin which is produced by fungi;
  2. Pathogenic fungi are divided into superficial fungi and deep fungi;
  3. Superficial fungi only invade the skin, hair and nails, like ringworm
  4. Deep fungi can invade human skin, mucous membranes, deep tissues and internal organs, and even cause disseminated infections throughout the body.
  5. Deep fungal infection of the intestine is manifested as fungal enteritis, which can exist independently, such as infantile Candida enteritis;
  6. One of the manifestations of systemic fungal infections, such as AIDS complicated by disseminated histoplasmosis.

Some fungi have also become important food sources.

  • There are more than 200 kinds of edible mushrooms, such as winter mushrooms, straw mushrooms, agaric, and cloud ears. And the empty shells of animals and plants after fungus intrusion, such as Cordyceps sinensis.
  • Other fungi are used in food processing, for example, yeast is used in bread and other processing, and fungi are also required for winemaking.
